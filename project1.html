    • E-Hop

    Project Description

    In the Fall of 2023, I took IAT267 and was assigned a final project in groups of 4 to create a smart home device, health monitoring device or an educational toy for children. Multiple sensor usage was mandatory, powered by Arduino Microcontroller and Processing to display the user interface. My group and I decided to make an educational toy for children called E-Hop, a digital iteration of the traditional hopscotch game.

    We as a group decided to go with E-Hop because we found that the first five years of a child are the most crucial stage for physical development. Moreover, five to six percent of children, which amounts to two children in every classroom in Canada, are affected by Developmental Coordination Disorder (DCD) or Dyspraxia. It is a condition where children are severely uncoordinated for their age. Through E-Hop, we aim to provide early intervention to minimize the risk of getting DCD for children prone to the disease in a fun and educative method.
